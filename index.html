Why Web Development?

From grade school on, I was always a big math nerd. It's always been my best subject academically speaking, and I have always had a passion for training my brain logically to solve problems, whether in a puzzle or a classroom.

Starting college as an astrophysics major at the University of Colorado - Boulder, I had high hopes of making a living as a theoretical astrophysicist, putting my natural curiosity and interest in applied mathematics to use. But before too long, I felt something was missing from my education: creativity. I eventually dropped the physics focus in favor of English and mathematics, and graduated with a BA in 2014.

As much as I enjoyed my education, I had no idea what I would use it for professionally. After teaching and tutoring for a few years, I got a part time job as the assistant to the CEO of a renewable energy tech company. After answering emails for a couple months, I was given more responsibility writing press releases, designing and writing email newsletters, and organizing the website via the company CMS.

After a year in that position, I was ready to put my love of logic and language to better use: I would challenge myself to learn more about the foundations of those web frameworks I had been using and discover how to bring those ideas to life on my own.
